Hello Google Cloud Platform Customer,

As you have already been notified earlier this year, your Google Cloud Platform (GCP) billing account(s) must be migrated to Google Cloud Korea LLC and KRW payments as the billing in your local currency, South Korean won (KRW) is now available. All customers with a South Korea address are required to be billed under Google Cloud Korea LLC.

We noticed that your account has not been migrated to your local currency, KRW. It’s important that you migrate your accounts to Google Cloud Korea LLC and KRW currency as soon as possible, since non-migrated accounts will be terminated by March 21, 2021.

Please note that your GCP resources or your pricing will not be affected by this migration, and will continue to run as usual.

What do I need to do?

Take a few minutes to migrate your GCP accounts, including any free tier or free trial accounts, to local currency payments. All customers with a South Korea address must do so to avoid future account termination.

To migrate your accounts, click on your GCP billing account(s) listed below and follow the instructions in your GCP console (you can find detailed instructions in the GCP Korea Migration FAQ listed below). If you are a registered business, you will need to have your business registration number and related tax information ready for tax-related purposes:

  • Billing Account: 01CDB7-731D1D-74DC29

What do I need to know?

When you migrate your GCP billing accounts:

  • Google Asia Pacific Pte Ltd will assign and transfer your Google Cloud Platform License Agreement and your Google Maps Platform License Agreement, if applicable, to Google Cloud Korea LLC, as Google Asia Pacific’s reseller. Your account information, including your email address and other contact information associated with it, will be transferred to Google Cloud Korea LLC.
  • Google Cloud Korea LLC will issue your invoices.
  • In accordance with local Korea laws, these invoices will be subject to Value Added Tax (VAT).
